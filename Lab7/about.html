About Me

This is what I feel.

Like many teenagers, my journey into the captivating world of Greek mythology began with a single book: Percy Jackson and the Lightning Thief. From the moment I cracked open its pages, I was transported into a realmtween myth and reality blurred.

Their actions, often driven by jealousy, pride, and lust, can be infuriatingly selfish and destructive. From Zeus's relentless infidelity to Hera's vengeful wrath, their behavior wreaks havoc on both mortals and immortals alike. Their actions are far from divine. Instead, they resemble petulant children, wielding power without restraint and causing chaos wherever they go.

The gods' struggles with power dynamics, familial conflicts, and personal flaws mirror our own complexities. Their emotions, whether love, anger, or jealousy, reflect the range of human feelings. Additionally, the consequences of their actions, both positive and negative, echo the ripple effects of our own choices .
